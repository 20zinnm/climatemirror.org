Climate Mirror

Mirrors

Climate Mirror is a distributed effort conducted by volunteers, in conjunction with efforts from institutions such as University of Pennsylvania, University of Toronto, and the Internet Archive.

The following sites are known to host mirrors of climate data:

  • climate.daknob.net - includes a list of known mirrored datasets from volunteers
  • climatetorrents.com
  • paleo.climatemirror.org

Partners

  • The PPEH Lab at the University of Pennsylvania runs the #DataRefuge project. The team there includes professional archivists and researchers, who are organizing efforts and working with the volunteers to standardize our approach to making backups and live mirrors.
  • The Environmental Data and Governance Initiative
  • We are also aware of and working in coordination with more established players in government data archiving, such as the Internet Archive and California Digital Library's End of Term Project.

Contributing a Mirror and Editing this Site

This site is a GitHub repository. If you've already filled out the form indicating you've finished mirroring a data repository, feel free to submit a Pull Request on GitHub with your changes (or get in touch on Slack) and we'll make the necessary changes to the site.

About This Site

Climate Mirror is part of an ad-hoc project to mirror public climate datasets before the Trump Administration takes office to make sure these datasets remain freely and broadly accessible. It was kicked off by a tweet from journalist Eric Holthaus, and similar efforts were started independently at the University of Toronto and University of Pennsylvania.
